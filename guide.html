Quick start guide

User Guide

Setup, usage, and troubleshooting for your Wave Clock.

Getting started

Set it up in three steps

Step 1

Power it on

Plug in the USB-C cable. The screen will briefly show your Wave Clock's name while it connects.

Step 2

Connect to your WiFi (first time only)

If this is a brand-new setup, your phone or computer will need to join a temporary network the device creates:

  • On your phone, go to WiFi settings and join the network named "WaveClock-Setup".
  • A setup page should open automatically. If it doesn't, open a web browser.
  • Select your home WiFi network and enter its password.
  • The Wave Clock will reconnect and continue setup automatically.
Step 3

Choose your town

The screen will show “WELCOME! Select your town.” Use the wheel (up/down) to scroll through the available beach towns, then press the bottom button to confirm. This step only happens once — your Wave Clock will remember it from now on.

That's it — your Wave Clock is ready and will show the Tide & Conditions screen.

The controls

Bottom button

Hold top button (5 sec)

The five screens

What it cycles through

Your Wave Clock cycles through these automatically (you control how often, in Settings), or you can flip through them manually anytime.

  • Tide & Conditions — Today's tide curve, water & air temp, wind, and sky — the default screen.
  • Wave Forecast — 5-day wave height chart, shaded by quality: Clean, Fair, or Choppy.
  • Weather Forecast — 5-day high/low temps, wind, sky icons, and a rain chart.
  • Sunset — Shows automatically for the hour before sunset each day — illustrated countdown, no need to go looking for it.
  • Lifeguard / Surf Report — Rip current risk, swell detail, wave/wind/temp readings, and the next tide.
A note on the Sunset screen: for the hour before sunset, your Wave Clock automatically switches to it and stays there — the top button won't move away from it during that window (this is intentional, so you never miss it). The wheel can still peek at other screens, but it'll return to Sunset on its own after about a minute.

Settings

Press the bottom button to open

Press it again to move to the next Settings page, and once more to close.

  • Screen Cycle — how often the display automatically advances: Off, 1 Minute, or 5 Minutes.
  • Location — change which town's data your Wave Clock shows. Use the wheel to browse (all towns are shown at once, so you can see how far to scroll), then confirm with the bottom button.

Troubleshooting

If something's off

A small warning triangle appears in the corner of the screen

This means your Wave Clock hasn't successfully updated its data in a few hours — usually a WiFi issue. Check the Diagnostics screen below for more detail.

Checking what's wrong: the Diagnostics screen

Hold the top button for 5 seconds. This shows firmware version, WiFi connection status and signal strength, your selected town, how long since data last updated successfully, and how long it's been running. Press any button to close it.

Checking for a software update manually

Your Wave Clock checks for updates automatically once a day. To check right away instead: open Diagnostics (hold the top button 5 seconds), then press the wheel. It'll show its progress and let you know the result.

Need to switch to a different WiFi network

Unplug the Wave Clock. Hold the bottom button down, then plug it back in while still holding the button. This clears the saved WiFi network only — your town, screen settings, and everything else stay exactly as you left them. Follow the WiFi setup steps again once it restarts.

The screen seems frozen or stuck

  • Check the Diagnostics screen first — it'll show if WiFi is actually connected.
  • Unplug and replug the power cable — this simply restarts the device and doesn't erase anything.
  • If it still doesn't recover, reach out for support — have the Diagnostics screen's info ready.
