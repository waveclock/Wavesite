Quick start guide

User Guide

Setup, usage, and troubleshooting for your Wave Clock.

Getting started

Set it up in three steps

Step 1

Plug it in

Just plug in the USB-C cable — it starts connecting right away.

Step 2

Join your WiFi (first time only)

On your phone, join the network called "WaveClock-Setup". A setup page will pop up — just pick your home WiFi, enter the password, and your Wave Clock takes it from there.

Step 3

Pick your town

Scroll to your beach town with the wheel and press the bottom button. You'll only do this once.

That's it — your Wave Clock is ready to go.

Design your own

Fun way to show what you want

No app needed.

Watching a photo turn into a hand-drawn sketch right in front of you never gets old. Your display shows whatever you want — from special events to tonight's dinner plans.

Turn on the Custom screen in Settings, then just look at it — you'll see a QR code to scan right there. Already have something published? Press the wheel anytime to bring the QR code back up.

The six screens

What it cycles through

It cycles through these on its own, or flip through them anytime.

  • Tide & Conditions — today's tide, temps, wind, and sky. The default screen.
  • Wave Forecast — five-day wave heights, rated Clean, Fair, or Choppy.
  • Weather Forecast — five-day highs, lows, and rain.
  • Sunset — by default, pops up on its own about 30 minutes before sunset, then returns to normal once it's passed. Fully configurable in Settings — including turning it off, or having it always available to browse to.
  • Lifeguard / Surf Report — rip current risk, swell, and the next tide.
  • Custom — draw, write, stamp an icon, or upload a photo (auto-converted to line art) — all from your phone.
About Sunset: by default, it takes over about 30 minutes before sunset so you don't miss it. Adjust the timing — or turn it off entirely — in Settings.

Settings

Press the bottom button to open

Press it again to flip through the pages, and once more to close.

  • Screen Cycle — how often it auto-advances: Off, 1 Minute, or 5 Minutes.
  • Location — change your town anytime.
  • Screens — turn any screen on or off (Wave Forecast, Weather, Lifeguard, Custom, Sunset). Wheel to pick one, press to toggle it.
  • Sunset Reminder — how long before sunset it pops up: None, 30 Minutes, or 60 Minutes.

Troubleshooting

If something's off

A small warning triangle shows up

Just a WiFi hiccup — hold the top button for 5 seconds to check its connection.

Checking for a software update

It checks on its own every night. To check right now: hold the top button 5 seconds, then press the wheel.

Holding the top button for 5 seconds also shows technical details (firmware version, WiFi signal, device ID) — handy to have on hand if you ever need to reach out for support.

Switching to a different WiFi network

Unplug it, hold the bottom button, and plug it back in while still holding it. This only forgets your WiFi — everything else stays put. Then set up WiFi again like the first time.

The screen seems frozen or stuck

  • Hold the top button 5 seconds to check its WiFi connection.
  • Unplug and plug it back in — nothing gets erased.
  • Still stuck? Reach out to us.
